VANI Silent Speech Device

Project Details

VANI is a Silent Speech Recognition (SSR) device designed to restore communication for laryngeal and speech-impaired patients by translating silent articulatory muscle movements into audible speech. The system leverages advanced Electromyography (EMG) to capture muscle activation patterns associated with speech production, even in the absence of vocal sound. By combining EMG signal processing with machine learning algorithms, VANI decodes silent speech intent and converts it into synthesized voice output, enabling patients to communicate naturally and independently. The device focuses on non-invasive sensing, real-time performance, and clinical applicability. Key Features EMG-based detection of silent speech muscle movements Real-time conversion of silent speech into audible output Machine learning–driven signal classification and speech mapping Non-invasive, wearable design suitable for clinical and daily use Innovation & Impact VANI represents a significant advancement in the integration of biomedical signal acquisition and machine learning, addressing a critical gap in assistive communication technologies. By eliminating the dependency on vocal cord function, the system offers a promising solution for: Post-laryngectomy patients Individuals with vocal fold paralysis Neurogenic speech impairments
