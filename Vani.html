VANI Silent Speech Device

Project Overview

VANI is a Silent Speech Recognition (SSR) device designed to restore communication for laryngeal and speech-impaired patients by translating silent articulatory muscle movements into audible speech. The system leverages advanced Electromyography (EMG) to capture muscle activation patterns associated with speech production, even in the absence of vocal sound.

By combining EMG signal processing with machine learning algorithms, VANI decodes silent speech intent and converts it into synthesized voice output, enabling patients to communicate naturally and independently. The device focuses on non-invasive sensing, real-time performance, and clinical applicability.

Key Features

  • EMG-based detection of silent speech muscle movements.
  • Real-time conversion of silent signals into synthesized audible output.
  • Machine learning–driven signal classification and speech mapping.
  • Non-invasive, wearable design suitable for clinical and daily use.

Clinical Impact

Eliminating dependency on vocal cord function, VANI offers a promising solution for:

  • Post-laryngectomy patients.
  • Individuals with vocal fold paralysis.
  • Patients suffering from neurogenic speech impairments.
