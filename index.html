About Me

I’m a healthcare technology researcher and biodesign fellow at School of International Biodesign AIIMS Delhi, working on translating unmet clinical needs into affordable, scalable medical devices. My background spans biomedical sensing, embedded systems, and human-centered design, with hands-on experience in neurorehabilitation, assistive wearables, and biosignal processing (EMG, IMU, EOG).

Previously, I served as a Junior Research Fellow at IIT Delhi, where I developed smart rehabilitation systems for stroke patients and conducted hospital-based testing in collaboration with AIIMS. I’m also the founder of Brainovative Labs, leading nationally awarded projects in silent speech recognition and biomechanics sensing, supported by government innovation grants. I hold an MCA and BCA, and my work is driven by a strong focus on building clinically validated, real-world healthcare solutions at the intersection of research, engineering, and product development.
